Stan Lee

Excelsior! The man behind Marvel.

Here's a time line of Stan Lee's life:

  • 1922 - Birth Date: Born Stanley Martin Lieber on 28th December, at his parents' Manhattan apartment.

  • 1929 - The Great Depression era: During the Great Depression, hundreds of families were impacted by the stock market disaster. Stan worked as an usher at a movie theatre part-time to support his family.

  • 1938 - Teenage Years: At the age of sixteen and a half, Stan completed high school early in 1939, and he then joined the WPA Federal Theatre Project.

  • 1939 - Starting Career: As a writer, Stan later abbreviated his name to "Stan Lee." With the assistance of his uncle, he began working for Timely Comics as an office assistant in 1939, and in the early 1940s, he was promoted to interim editor. During World War II, Lee also participated in domestic Army service as a writer and illustrator.

  • 1947 - Marriage: On December 5, 1947, Stan wed Newcastle, England native Joan Clayton Boocock.

  • 1960 - Creating superheros with Kirby: The Fantastic Four were the first superheroes Lee and illustrator Jack Kirby produced jointly. Due to the team's immediate success, Lee and Marvel's illustrators created a flood of new books. Lee co-created the Hulk, Thor, Iron Man, and the X-Men alongside Kirby once more.

  • 1962 - Spiderman! The The birth of Spider-Man! Stan created the iconic superhero in 1962.

  • 1964 - Captain America makes a return! Captain America returns more even more popular than he ever was before, 20 years ago.

  • 1966 - Black Panther makes his debut! The superhero Black Panther made his debut in Marvel Comics' Fantastic Four #52 as a guest character.

  • 1967 - December debuted Capitain Marvel!In December 1967, Marvel Super-Heroes No. 12 featured the character's debut.

  • 1969 - September 1969: Falcon! The Falcon, Captain America's sidekick and the second ever black superhero, made his debut.

  • 2001 - Co-founds POW! Entertainment POW! (Purveyors of Wonder) Entertainment was founded by Lee, Gill Champion, and Arthur Lieberman to create properties for movies, television shows, and video games.

  • 2009 - National Arts Medal Stan Lee received the National Arts Medal in 2008 from President Bush. He was recognised as one of the country's most active storytellers. He established the Stan Lee Foundation in 2009 to focus on literacy, education, and the arts.

  • 2016 - Stan Lee's God Woke Stan Lee's God Woke, a digital graphic novel with text that was initially created as a poem he read at Carnegie Hall in 1972, was debuted by Lee at the 2016 Comic-Con International.

  • 2018 - Death: dies at the age of 95 in Los Angeles, of heart and respiratory failure, six weeks before his 96th birthday.
